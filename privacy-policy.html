Legal & Security

Privacy Policy

Your data stays on your machine. Local-first, offline, and secure.

URL Traversal Timer does not sell, track, or share user data with third parties. This extension is designed to run entirely locally in your browser and use only the data needed to cycle through a user-defined list of URLs at a chosen interval.

Information We Collect

The extension may store the following configuration data locally on your device:

  • URLs you enter into the extension queue
  • The active tab URL used to initialize page-specific lists
  • Traversal state configurations (running state, pause state, and interval selections)

How We Use This Information

We use this information strictly to provide the extension’s core utility functions:

  • Saving your URL list and loop settings between browser restarts
  • Cycling the active browser tab through your selected URLs
  • Maintaining pause, resume, and stop controls across session states

Where the Data Is Stored

All configuration and list settings are kept locally in Chrome’s secure extension storage on your device (using chrome.storage.local). It is never transmitted or uploaded to any developer server, analytics service, or third-party backend.

Data Sharing

We do not sell, transfer, disclose, or share any user data, except where explicitly required by law.

Required Browser Permissions

The extension requests Chrome browser permissions only for its stated, minimum-scope purposes:

  • storage to save your local URL queues and timing configurations.
  • tabs to read the active tab URL for list association and navigate pages.
  • alarms to keep timing calculations accurate in the background without draining battery life.

Children’s Privacy

This extension is not intended for children under 13, and we do not knowingly collect or request personal information from children.

Changes to This Policy

If this privacy policy is revised in the future, we will update this document directly to reflect the updated version.
